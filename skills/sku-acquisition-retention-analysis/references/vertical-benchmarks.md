# 2026 Ecommerce Retention Benchmarks by Vertical

Use this reference when contextualizing a store's single-purchase rate and repeat purchaser count in the Budget Allocation Signal section of the Merchandising Brief. Do not present these as guarantees. Present them as directional context: "stores in this category typically see X; yours is currently Y."

---

## Annual Repeat Rate and 12-Month Cohort Retention

| Vertical | Annual Repeat Rate | 12-Month Cohort Retention | Repurchase Window | Merchandising Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beauty / Skincare | 30% - 45% | 25% - 40% | 6 - 10 weeks | Auto-replenishment; routine-based bundles |
| Fashion / Apparel | 20% - 35% | 18% - 30% | 8 - 16 weeks | Seasonal collection drops; cross-sell by style |
| Food and Beverage | 35% - 50% | 30% - 45% | 2 - 4 weeks | Bulk purchase incentives; high-frequency reorder flows |
| Supplements / Health | 30% - 42% | 25% - 38% | 4 - 8 weeks | Subscription conversion; replenishment reminders |
| Home / Premium Goods | 10% - 20% | 8% - 15% | 12 - 24 months | VIP treatment; accessories for high-ticket core units |
| Pet Products | 30% - 45% | 25% - 40% | 3 - 6 weeks | Subscription hooks; breed or size-specific bundles |
| Electronics / Gadgets | 12% - 22% | 10% - 18% | 6 - 18 months | Accessory cross-sell; warranty or protection plan upsell |

## LTV:CAC Ratio Benchmarks (2026)

| Status | LTV:CAC Ratio | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum viable | 3:1 | CAC is offset, but thin margin for error |
| Healthy | 4:1 - 5:1 | Sustainable unit economics for paid acquisition |
| Strong | > 5:1 | Capacity to scale paid acquisition aggressively |
| At risk | < 3:1 | Acquisition spend is eroding profit; prioritize retention immediately |

The 3:1 minimum reflects the 2026 average DTC customer acquisition cost of $120 - $210. At $150 CAC, a store needs $450 in LTV per acquired customer to break even on paid acquisition.

## How to Use This Reference

In the Budget Allocation Signal section of the Merchandising Brief:

1. Identify the store's vertical from the product category the user provided in Turn 1.
2. Compare the store's `single_purchase_rate` (from `data_quality`) against the expected annual repeat rate range.
3. State the comparison plainly: "Your current single-purchase rate of X% implies a Y% repeat rate, which is [above / within / below] the Z% - W% range typical for [vertical] stores."
4. If the store is below the low end of the range, flag this as a retention gap and strengthen the Retention Flow Recommendations accordingly.
5. If the store is above the high end of the range, note the advantage and recommend protecting those retention-driver SKUs from price erosion or channel cannibalization.

Do not use these benchmarks as thresholds for the Product Role Map classifications. Those use `acquisition_index` and `retention_index` from the script. Benchmarks are for contextualizing the store's overall health, not for individual SKU classification.
